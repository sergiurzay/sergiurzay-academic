abstract: >
  We present a theoretical framework that explains how European citizens form their preferences
  on EU integration. Building on theories of nation formation, we consider three distinct
  ideological cost functions measuring the impact of EU legislation on national politics.
  Individual-level survey data from EU member states show that European citizens tend to be
  highly sophisticated: support for integration is driven by citizens who believe EU legislation
  amends their national policy, while Euroscepticism emerges among those who see it as
  interference.
